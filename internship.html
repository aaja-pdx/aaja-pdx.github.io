AAJA-SPJ Emerging Northwest Journalists Internship

Update: The Oregonian/OregonLive will be hosting

Applications are now open for the 2020 Emerging Northwest Journalists Internship, created by AAJA-Portland and SPJ Oregon Territory Chapter to promote underrepresented voices in Pacific Northwest newsrooms.

Applicants must be a college sophomore, junior, senior or graduate student majoring in journalism, or who aspire to a career in journalism. Applicants must reside in or attend school in Oregon or Southwest Washington. Applicants who previously lived in Oregon or Southwest Washington will also be considered. Applicants must have a valid driver's license.

The selected intern will be assigned to The Oregonian/OregonLive, who will provide a staff mentor for the duration of the 10-week internship. The selected intern will be paid at least the prevailing regional minimum wage.

Our 2019 intern, Donald Orr, a recent graduate of Oregon State University, is wrapping up his internship at Oregon Public Broadcasting. Here’s what he has to say:

“This internship has blessed me with the opportunity to be part of an inclusive, professional newsroom where I get to hone my craft as a journalist daily. Hearing my voice on public radio has been a goal of mine for a long time—to have that now be a reality is truly surreal. I’m eternally grateful to AAJA-Portland, SPJ Oregon Territory Chapter and Oregon Public Broadcasting for continuing to elevate diverse voices like mine, and giving me the opportunity to grow as a journalist—I hope to pay it forward someday. Thank you!”

This application will require:

  • A cover letter (We welcome your thoughts on any topic, but we would particularly like to read about your experiences with diversity and journalism.)
  • A resume
  • Up to five work samples
  • Up to three references with contact information

Please gather these materials before you begin. You can apply using this form by Sunday, Nov. 10.
